The Workshop

The Workbench

The "tiny and well equipped" workbench consists of a wide range of hardware tools to more delicate electronics equipment, such as embedded programming tools (e.g. STLINK-V2MINIE).

The most used tools being the 3D-printer, soldering station, DIY bench power supply, multimeter, debugging tools, other programmers, and a simple logic analyzer.

In addition the workbench has a small range of ready to use microcontrollers and electronic components for rapid prototyping, and storage for ongoing projects.

3D-Printer: Bambu Lab P1P

The first proper 3D-printer in the house (the predecessor was a rather disastrous M3D Micro 3D-printer, Kickstarter funded in 2015).

The P1P was purchased before the P1S was publicly announced, hence the unoffical enclosure solution, which required it's own 3D-printed parts. Finally, I can print ABS and PETG without any warping, and ensure consistent prints.

NB! Due to ABS printing, notice the IKEA air filter and air quality sensor light in green.
